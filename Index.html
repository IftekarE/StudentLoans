Fighting an uphill battle

What are student loans? It is a parasite that sticks with its host for their entire life. It is hard to get rid off it is mentally taxing and limits a person’s ability to flourish economically; it takes its toll on a person in more ways than one.

Towards the end of 2018, the collective federal student debt was $1.47 trillion, that’s more than auto and credit loans. 44.7 million people in the united states have student loans, that’s 1 in every 5 adults. The loan holder demographic consists of the majority being under 30, and senior citizens the age of 60 and over make a significant portion of the loans as well. A reason for this immense age gap is because of high-interest rates often being as high as 13%, on top of that the interests are compounded; most people 7 years after graduating owe more money than originally borrowed.
